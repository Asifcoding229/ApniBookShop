Time Management Tips for SAT Preparation

Preparing for the SAT can be challenging, but effective time management can make a significant difference in your performance. The SAT is a timed test, so it's essential to learn how to pace yourself to maximize your score. Below are some practical and actionable tips to help you manage your time wisely.

Why is Time Management Important?

The SAT consists of four sections, each with strict time limits:

  • Reading Test: 65 minutes for 52 questions
  • Writing and Language Test: 35 minutes for 44 questions
  • Math (No Calculator): 25 minutes for 20 questions
  • Math (Calculator Allowed): 55 minutes for 38 questions
With limited time, learning to work efficiently is crucial to completing the test and answering as many questions as possible correctly.

Top Time Management Tips

Here are the best strategies for managing your time during SAT preparation and on test day:

  • Understand the Format: Familiarize yourself with the test structure, types of questions, and time limits for each section.
  • Practice with Timed Tests: Take full-length SAT practice tests under timed conditions to simulate the real exam.
  • Skip and Return: If a question is taking too long, skip it and come back to it later if time allows.
  • Use Process of Elimination: Narrow down your choices to save time when you're unsure of the correct answer.
  • Keep an Eye on the Clock: Be aware of how much time is left but avoid obsessively checking the clock.
  • Set a Pacing Goal: Divide the total time by the number of questions in each section to determine how much time to spend on each question.

Final Thoughts

Time management is a skill that can be mastered with consistent practice. By using these strategies, you can improve your pacing and feel more confident on test day. Remember, preparation is key, so make sure to include timed practice sessions in your study plan.
